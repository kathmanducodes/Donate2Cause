Our Process

How We Verify Campaigns

1

Identity Verification

Campaign organizers must submit government-issued identification (Citizenship Certificate, Passport, or Driver's License). Our team verifies the authenticity of these documents and matches them against the applicant.

  • Valid Nepali ID (for Nepal-based organizers)
  • Valid passport (for diaspora organizers)
  • Selfie with ID for face verification
2

Need Assessment

We evaluate the legitimacy and urgency of the cause through detailed documentation and third-party verification.

  • Medical reports and cost estimates (for medical campaigns)
  • School/institution documents (for education campaigns)
  • Damage assessments and quotes (for disaster relief)
  • Community impact statements
3

Beneficiary Confirmation

We directly contact the intended beneficiary (or their legal guardian) to confirm their consent and need.

  • Direct phone/video call with beneficiary
  • Written consent from beneficiary or guardian
  • Verification of bank account ownership
4

Campaign Approval

Once all checks pass, our verification committee reviews the application and grants final approval.

  • Multi-stage internal review
  • Cross-reference with previous campaigns
  • Final approval by verification team lead

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the verification process take?

Typically 2-5 business days. Complex cases may take longer. We work diligently to verify campaigns while maintaining accuracy.

What happens if my campaign fails verification?

We provide detailed feedback on what was missing or needed. You can resubmit with the required information.

Is my personal information safe?

Yes. We use industry-standard encryption and security measures. Your data is only used for verification purposes and is never shared with third parties without consent.

Can I report a suspicious campaign?

Absolutely. If you see anything suspicious, please contact us immediately at support@donate2cause.com. We take all reports seriously.
